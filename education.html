My Education Journey

Starting from Zero

I began my tech journey at Kirinyaga University studying Software Engineering with absolutely no background in technology. No prior coding experience, no tech subjects in high school just curiosity (rumours that engineers have sleepless nights when coding).

The first semester was overwhelming. Everyone seemed to know terms and concepts I'd never heard before. I was "blank on where to start" and found it "hard and confusing to follow a path."

The Learning Breakthrough

I realized tech isn't about knowing everything from the start. It's about embracing the confusion, asking questions, and building one concept at a time. I started making learning interesting by working on small projects that mattered to me.

What I'm Learning Now

Currently, I'm focusing on both software engineering fundamentals and data analytics:

Front-end Development React & JavaScript Data Visualization API Integration Statistics

Why Statistics and Data?

I discovered I love statistics and understanding "why things or rather data are happening the way they are." There's something powerful about getting to the root of problems and finding patterns in data.

This interest in data analysis complements my software engineering studies perfectly - I can build the tools AND analyze what they reveal.

Current Projects

  • Weather Dashboard: A React app that's challenging with evolving languages
  • Number Converter: Practical tool to understand different data representations
  • Data Analysis Exercises: Working with real datasets to find insights
  • This Portfolio: Applying what I learn to build something meaningful

Learning Philosophy

I believe in "learning and relearning." Technology evolves fast, and what matters isn't memorizing everything, but developing the skills to learn whatever comes next.

Most importantly: "You must make what you're doing interesting so that you do your best." This mindset keeps me engaged even when concepts are challenging.
