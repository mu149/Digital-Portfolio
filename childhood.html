My Childhood

A Normal Girl with Adaptable Spirit

I grew up as a normal girl who believed I could adapt to working in any field except medicine. That adaptability has been my greatest strength, allowing me to explore different interests and find my path.

Finding My Path

I never did any tech subjects until university. Coming to campus, I was completely blank on where to start. The tech world seemed overwhelming and confusing to follow a path. There were so many languages, frameworks, and directions it was hard to know where to begin.

The Turning Point

The breakthrough came when I stopped trying to follow everyone else's path and started creating my own. I learned that in technology, it's okay to start confused , everyone does. What matters is starting.

Early Values That Shaped Me

Adaptability Curiosity Persistence Learning Mindset Finding Joy in Work

Why This Matters Now

My childhood taught me that confusion is just part of the journey. Starting software engineering with zero background was intimidating, but that early lesson about making things interesting kept me going. Now, I'm loving it

That normal girl who could adapt to anything learned that tech isn't about knowing everything from the start. It's about being willing to learn, relearn, and find the interesting parts that keep you engaged.
